02 — Logos

Three directions. One mark to pick.

All three keep the spirit of the original circle monogram: a contained, confident letterform pair. Each solves a different problem with the current mark — the weight collision between H and J, the optical imbalance at small sizes, and the missing room for a wordmark lockup. Recommendation noted on each.

A · Recommended

Refined circle — lowercase hj

Same container as v1, but swapped to lowercase hj set tight in Open Sans 800. Fixes the H/J stroke collision, reads cleaner at 16px, and the dot-over-the-j gives it quiet personality. Primary mark.

B · Editorial

Bracket mark — [hj]

A byline-shaped container. Brackets nod to editing, code, and citations — the three currencies of the practice. Horizontal, so it lockups cleanly with a wordmark. Use where the circle feels too consumer.

C · Wordmark-first

Serif period — hj.

A sentence, not a symbol. Bitter 700 with a blue period — ends the thought, names the owner. Strongest on long-form surfaces: essays, decks, email sign-offs. Pair with mark A for favicon use.

04 — Type

Same stack, tighter job descriptions.

Open Sans for UI and body — its lightest weights carry more of the display load than they do today. Bitter is the editorial voice — used sparingly, always for a phrase, never a full paragraph. Source Code Pro is the metadata voice — labels, tags, timestamps, never copy.

06 — Rules

Four rules. Everything else is a judgment call.

Fewer rules, better enforcement. If a use passes these four, it passes.

DO

Use Signal blue on Paper or Ink — the three tested pairings only.

DON'T

Recolor the mark to match a campaign, portfolio piece, or season.

DO

Keep the mark fully contained inside its circle — nothing bleeds.

DON'T

Add shadows, glows, bevels, or 3D. The mark is flat by design.
